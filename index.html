Overview

MC-SEARCH is the first comprehensive benchmark designed to evaluate Multimodal Agentic Retrieval-Augmented Generation (MM-RAG). Unlike traditional RAG, MC-SEARCH focuses on structured long reasoning chains where agents must navigate through vast knowledge bases (389K images and 784K documents) to solve complex queries.

Each instance provides step-wise sub-questions, explicit modality annotations (image vs. text), and intermediate reasoning states, enabling fine-grained analysis of agent planning and execution.


Figure 1. Reasoning topologies of MC-SEARCH.

We introduce five representative reasoning topologies mirroring real-world search patterns. Models are evaluated using Hit-per-Step (HPS), Rollout Deviation (RD), and Planning Accuracy to diagnose where agentic reasoning breaks down.
